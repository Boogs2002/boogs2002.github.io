𓁷 Origins Profile & Background

My journey into engineering didn't begin on a screen—it began with blueprints. At NATIN, I discovered a passion for structural design, utilizing AutoCAD to plan, scale, and map out physical houses. This early immersion in structural geometry shaped how I view the digital world. To me, a webpage isn't just code; it is a digital structure requiring pristine foundations, balanced grids, and exceptional spatial awareness.

Today, as a Software Engineering student at UNASAT, I have translated those architectural principles into Full-Stack Web Development and Software Security. Whether collaborating with a team to build an award-winning, first-place automatic water-garbage prototype using Arduino, or auditing environments for security flaws, I treat code as an enduring artifact built to last.

As a frontend freelancer and full-stack creator, I dedicate myself to bridging the gap between backend logic and beautiful, user-centered interfaces.
